<h1><center>Region Drafts</center></h1>
***
This document contains some simple descriptions of the visual profile and "overall vibe" of the different regions of Eiare. This is just a quick draft, for referential purposes. More elaborate descriptions go in their appropriate sector.

### Lumina
   - "Sparkly and shiny, brimming with nature and wonder, with traces of tragedy scattered throughout"
   - I think Lumina should be this thing that appears to be beautiful and magical at first glance, but when you look deeper, you realize it's the overgrowth of a war-torn land poisoned by corruption. I hope it will inspire a sense of wanting to bring it back to its full, lush glory.
   - To this end, Oscura serves a fantastic purpose in showing that Lumina is "losing its light."

### Avalia 
   - "Regal and elegant, prosperous city invocative of a fantasy version of the Roman Empire and the ancient Greeks, utterly consumed by corruption"
   - Avalia should be just as ugly as it is beautiful, I think, and having such a stark divide between the slums and the upper districts visually will not just give us the opportunity to have diverse areas, but also to showcase what this totalitarian elitism has done to the people. Getting to see both sides and fight against the oppressor after being oppressed yourself will be a great experience, I think. It should be immediately obvious upon entering these areas that one is rich and the other is poor.
   
### Exulas
   - "Forlorn and tranquil, the remains of a land which may once have thrived, but now tries only to survive. Dark and serene, haunted by forsaken hopes and forgotten dreams, with only the faintest glimmer of life still refusing to give out completely."
   - Exulas is meant to be an example of what Lumina will eventually become, if left unchecked. To this end it should be like the opposite of Lumina; dark and murky, with very little fancy visuals. It should feel dreary and depressing to be in, like trudging through muck—but one should be able to find the occasional sparkling oasis, showing that hope is scarce but present.
   - Oscura and Exulas should probably have a lot of commonality, visually.
     
### Malicis (Miasma)
   - "Psychedelic, disorienting, and existential; a journey through the senses of Self and Other. Bright and saturated neon nightmares, and logic be damned."
   - Miasma Malicis is _the_ place to shove all our wackiest designs and craziest ideas. It's an opportunity for us to completely stray from the visual design of every other area. Every crazy shader or nightmarish entity we come up with will inevitably find a home in here.
  - I have already done a great deal of research into how we can achieve amazing things for this place. We can even allow Umbra to move in 4D, for just this one area. Many other games have achieved this and the approach is well documented. Fractal shaders, and techniques like changing the value for pi, are all fantastic ways to achieve a consistent yet freakish results.

### Malicis Archipelago
   - "A meagre ensemble of isles with scant greenery upon which one can observe the skyline of what once was."
   - To compensate for Malicis Miasma being an entire area that gets removed from the game after completion, Malicis itself has to be fairly small and simple. 
   - But we can make use of the shape of the islands to allow this to be a place from where you can view the entirety of the crescent-shaped Avalia.

### Elvira
   - "An underground technological wasteland. The clear remains of what once was a civilisation far more advanced than whatever could be found on Avalia, actively being consumed by the maw of the seas."
   - Elvira can be distinct by virtue of having Esterian technology, since Elvira was an Esterian city. We can showcase here just how much valuable stuff was lost by the calamities, and it will give players a sense of scale for the damages done.

### The Pristine Castle
   - "A shining palace of crisp whites, rich blacks, and voluptuous golden arches adorning crystalline, sky-blue ornamentation."
   - The Pristine Castle is meant to be the peak of Avalian architecture. All previously mentioned features of Avalia, how they exude the grandiosity of ancient Greco-Roman architectural traits, are turned up to 11, with a clear religious influence and a sense of divinity about it all.
   - It should be immaculate, completely obfuscating the rampant corruption and malice of the people within it. There should be no hint that any wrongdoings occur here at all, as these are all masked to be underground, or beyond the city walls.
