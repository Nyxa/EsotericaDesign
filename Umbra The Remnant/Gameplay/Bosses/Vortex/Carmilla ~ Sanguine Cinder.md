### Ability Breakdown
**Carmilla** is, in actuality, **Akhanti**, the daughter of **Igneous Lord Niandes** and his wife, **Chelnayah**, both worshiped by the **Niandes** tribe after attaining mythical status over creating their fabled golden **Heartflame**, and azure **Soulflame**, also known as the **Divine Embers**.

**Akhanti** however, had easily mastered both her parents' flames, and felt discontent with their limits. So she created her own flame; a flame with enhanced properties compared to its predecessors, but that used *blood* as fuel in addition to **Qualia**. This is **Akhanti**'s scarlet **Bloodflame**, a technique which led to the creation of the **Bloodwings** found in **Exulas**, where **Carmilla** also resides.

As a result of her backstory, **Carmilla** fights with all three **Divine Embers**. The player has, at this point, had many opportunities to become acquainted with the appropriate response to each **Ember**:

- *Golden* **Heartflame** attacks require any **Strike** to collide with them at the right time, which cancels them out and inflicts a bit of damage on **Carmilla**. 
	- A **Heavy Attack** landing at the right time will inflict *triple damage* on **Carmilla**, but **Umbra** will receive *double damage* if it's mistimed.
- *Azure* **Soulflame** attacks require a **Parry**, and inflict a bit of **Resilience** damage on **Carmilla**. 
	- A failed **Parry** will inflict *double* **Resilience** damage to **Umbra**.
	- Not blocking at all will additionally inflict significant damage to **Umbra**.
	- A **Perfect Parry** will inflict damage on **Carmilla**, on top of doing *enhanced* **Resilience** damage.
- *Scarlet* **Bloodflame** attacks require a **Dodge**. 
	- An *Energy Attack* whose **Active Frames** collide with a **Bloodflame** attack will cancel both attacks out.
		- There are no additional benefits for doing this, but it may be useful in a pinch.



### Narrative Summary
**Carmilla** invites **Umbra** over to dinner to get to know her, as **Umbra** has become a celebrity around these parts.

However, it turns out to be a ploy to devour Umbra and her friends, a practice Carmilla apparently engages in quite frequently.

Cut from a similar cloth as **Necroxia**, **Carmilla** follows her own rules and morals, and will kill for hedonistic reasons, as well as toy with her prey. **Umbra** and her squad are no exception.

After capturing them, she plays cruel games with them for entertainment, ending with something horrible happening to **Nemi**, who dies as a result.

Under threat of the same happening to **Litchi**, **Umbra** is put in a fancy dress and made to *"dance to the death"* with **Carmilla**, who fights **Umbra** to the rhythm of the song, and punishes her very hard for going off-rhythm.
