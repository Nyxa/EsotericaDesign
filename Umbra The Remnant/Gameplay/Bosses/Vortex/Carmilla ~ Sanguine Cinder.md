### Ability Breakdown
**Carmilla** is, in actuality, **Akhanti**, the daughter of **Igneous Lord Niandes** and his wife, **Chelnayah**, both worshiped by the **Niandes** tribe after attaining mythical status over creating their fabled golden **Heartflame**, and azure **Soulflame**, also known as the **Divine Embers**.

**Akhanti** however, had easily mastered both her parents' flames, and felt discontent with their limits. So she created her own flame; a flame with enhanced properties compared to its predecessors, but that used *blood* as fuel in addition to **Qualia**. This is **Akhanti**'s scarlet **Bloodflame**, a technique which led to the creation of the **Bloodwings** found in **Exulas**, where **Carmilla** also resides.

As a result of her backstory, **Carmilla** fights with all three **Divine Embers**. The player has, at this point, had many opportunities to become acquainted with the appropriate response to each **Ember**:

- *Golden* **Heartflame** attacks require any **Strike** to collide with them at the right time, which cancels them out and inflicts a bit of damage on **Carmilla**. 
	- A **Heavy Attack** landing at the right time will inflict *triple damage* on **Carmilla**, but **Umbra** will receive *double damage* if it's mistimed.
- *Azure* **Soulflame** attacks require a **Parry**, and inflict a bit of **Resilience** damage on **Carmilla**. 
	- A failed **Parry** will inflict *double* **Resilience** damage to **Umbra**.
	- Not blocking at all will additionally inflict significant damage to **Umbra**.
	- A **Perfect Parry** will inflict damage on **Carmilla**, on top of doing *enhanced* **Resilience** damage.
- *Scarlet* **Bloodflame** attacks require a **Dodge**. 
	- An *Energy Attack* whose **Active Frames** collide with a **Bloodflame** attack will cancel both attacks out.
		- There are no additional benefits for doing this, but it may be useful in a pinch.

**Carmilla** uses *Twin Fans* as her weapon of choice, to propagate her flames.

**Umbra**, during this fight, has an *altered animation set*. Her inputs remain the same, but she will respond in tune with the song.

(Budget permitting) If the player has completed the **Avalian Phoenix Festival** and learned to dance from **Alace**, **Umbra**'s altered animations will differ slightly, as otherwise she'll learn to dance from **Litchi** instead.

### Narrative Summary
**Carmilla** invites **Umbra** over to dinner to get to know her, as **Umbra** has become a celebrity around these parts.

However, it turns out to be a ploy to devour Umbra and her friends, a practice Carmilla apparently engages in quite frequently.

Cut from a similar cloth as **Necroxia**, **Carmilla** follows her own rules and morals, and will kill for hedonistic reasons, as well as toy with her prey. **Umbra** and her squad are no exception.

After capturing them, she plays cruel games with them for entertainment, ending with something horrible happening to **Nemi**, who dies as a result.

Under threat of the same happening to **Litchi**, **Umbra** is put in a fancy dress and made to *"dance to the death"* with **Carmilla**, who fights **Umbra** to the rhythm of the song, and punishes her very hard for going off-rhythm.


### Phases

1. **Carmilla** fights to the rhythm of a *diegetic* song in a beautiful scarlet dance hall. After an introductory piece which sees them dancing, it evolves into rhythm-based combat. This phase sees Carmilla use only simple rhythms to get the player used to the dance combat.
2. Rhythm complexity is increased, and keeps increasing throughout this phase.
3. **Carmilla** becomes un-attackable and starts using **Bloodflame** attacks instead, which drain her healthbar by themselves. This phase requires pure dodging, until **Carmilla**'s health for the phase is fully depleted.
4. **Carmilla** breaks the speakers out of frustration, and starts flinging all *three* **Divine Embers** at **Umbra**. There is no rhythm based combat in this phase, and **Umbra**'s moveset returns to normal. However, the previous properties of the **Divine Embers** still apply. 

### Defeat
Upon being defeated by **Umbra**, **Carmilla** reveals that the dress she'd put **Umbra** in was an emergency failsafe. Essentially, she was guaranteed to win, and this entire thing was just a farce for her to toy with her prey before devouring them.

**Umbra** becomes trapped by the rigged dress, and **Carmilla** gloats a bit before throwing her finishing blow. However, before the hit lands, it is revealed that **Necroxia** has appeared, evidently protecting **Umbra**.

**Necroxia** proclaims that she initially took an interest in **Carmilla**, but quickly realized they were not cut from the same cloth. This event basically confirmed her suspicions. She thinks **Carmilla** ruined a perfectly beautiful artpiece by cheating. Claiming that an underhanded villain is a pathetic one, as villains ought to be imposing and worthy of respect.

After scolding **Carmilla**, **Necroxia** kills her in the name of "performing a quick art restoration." She then releases **Umbra** and hands her the keys to **Litchi**'s cell. When questioned why, she simply reaffirms what she'd told **Carmilla**.

Besides, **Umbra** couldn't die to the likes of that attack anyway. **Carmilla** was severely weakened. **Necroxia** was merely there for the drama. 

**Umbra** had won fair and square.
