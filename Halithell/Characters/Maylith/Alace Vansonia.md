### Repentance
After the [Destruction of the Pristine Castle](Alace%20Vansonia%20~%20Nightmare%20of%20Avalia.md#Narrative%20Summary), **Alace** spends a while by herself, ruminating. She eventually decides to own up to her mistakes and returns to the rubbles of **Avalia**, in order to help rebuild it.

The surviving citizens, however, are equal parts terrified of her, and angry at her. She is not welcomed at all. She offers to help rebuild the city, but the response is a resounding "no thanks;" something she expected and accepts. The residents do not trust her, and furthermore do not want their new home built by their former oppressor.

**Litchi**, however, does not, and opens her big mouth to protest. She argues that **Alace** may have done many bad things, but she's also ultimately the one who broke the status quo. If it weren't for her, they would've all still been living under the oppression of the **Avalian** regime. They don't have to forgive her, but to completely shut her out would not only be foolish, but also unfair.

The citizens are justly terrified of **Alace**, however. Her power has proven enough to topple the mighty **Esteria** in the past. What, then, is stopping her from simply taking control of the city whenever she so chooses? Her word?

Ultimately, the one to change the direction of things is **Umbra**. She argues that they are correct. They cannot stop her from taking power. And she understands that for her to help might be a slap in the face to the future residents of **New Avalia**; after all, they would have to live in a city built by the hands of one who once oppressed them.

Nevertheless, at the same time, without **Alace**, they are simply far weaker as a civilization, in a world that is already crumbling. To top it off, it sets a bad precedent for this new city that they do not allow her to even attempt at setting things straight. It is precisely because she's so powerful that they can be certain that this gesture is genuine. Nothing is stopping her from wiping what's left of the city, other than her own belief that she should repent and rebuild it instead.

When questioned if they should then just live at her whims, simply because she's powerful, **Umbra** responds by saying they have no choice in the matter. As far as they're concerned, **Alace** is a demigod. Such is the gap in their power. The fact she chooses not to hurt them is ultimately pure luck on their part, and it's no different for **Umbra**. **Umbra** understands **Alace**, because power begets distance, and no one wants to be lonely. **Alace** has simply chosen this to be the tribe she protects; out of free will, this time, rather than military compulsion.

They don't have to be grateful for it, but it remains a fact that they are simply lucky that their whims have swung this way. And they may as well make the best of the situation there is, instead of clinging to the past.

...Despite all this, many villagers still refuse, bringing up different occasions where **Alace** did bad things. This time, however, the one to interrupt them was none other than **Lette**. To the surprise of many, Lette is in favour of letting Alace help. She thinks it's time she paid her dues to the city. However, her condition is that **Alace** does everything by herself. The citizens will simply sit by and idly watch as **Alace** rebuilds the entire city on her own. If she accepts anyone's help, the deal is off.

Whilst **Litchi** wished to protest this punishment, **Alace** is quick to accept, and she gets to work on the same day.

And she keeps working, day after day, until before long, one house has been completely rebuilt.

And she keeps going, and going. Never once complaining or slowing down. And the villagers gradually find themselves trying to help her on instinct, which she always refuses.

Eventually, the one to break the deal is **Lette** herself. She admires **Alace**'s work ethic, and becomes convinced that she truly wishes to set things right. She makes it abundantly clear that she's not forgiving **Alace**, but she believes that good foundations are built on good premises, and watching any longer would be mean-spirited.

From then on, all the citizens start working together on the rebuild, including Umbra, until eventually they're left with a gorgeous city, named **New Avalia**.
